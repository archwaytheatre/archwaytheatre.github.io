Hamp is a drama set during the battle of Passchendaele during World War I.
When Private Hamp, who is a simplistic and guileless youth and one of the
smallest cogs in the war machine, crawls from a shell hole and walks away from
battle, the army finds itself compelled to notice him. He is court-martialled for
Desertion. While his defence, Lieutenant Hargreaves, and the Padre and others
try to make him realise the severity of the situation, his blind faith in their
ability and his insignificance give him hope.

This play is a brutal and honest look at the extremity of what war demands of
people not just at the front lines but those tasked with enforcing the laws and
regulations that are part of war.
